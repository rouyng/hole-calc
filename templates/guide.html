How to use this site

hole calc provides a simple calculator to determine the diameter of a hole/bore based on the size of three pin gauges (a.k.a. plug gauges or gages) that fit into it. This three-gauge method can provide a quick way to measure bore diameters when you don't have access to a GO/NOGO gauge or other precision bore measuring method. Measurements obtained via the three-gauge method should be considered approximate relative to the use of these other methods.

Precautions

hole calc simply does the math for you- it does not substitute for an understanding of the metrology involved in measuring holes. A full overview of how to use pin gauges and measure holes is outside the scope of this guide (here is a good start). However, the following caveats should be kept in mind when attempting to do precision work using the three-pin method:

  • Every gauge pin has a class indicating the tolerance of the pin diameter and are designated "+" or "-". The tolerance of each pin and resulting stackup will affect the measurement of the hole.
  • As with the standard GO/NOGO method of measuring a bore, surface roughness, (lack of) edge breaks and material will all affect how easily gauges slide into the bore and therefore the choice of gauges used for the final measurement.
  • The three gauge method assumes the bore to be perfectly round. If the bore is oval-shaped or lobed, the three gauge method will provide inconsistent results depending on the relative position of the gauges within the bore.
